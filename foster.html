Mission

Our mission is to improve animal sheltering and welfare and promote the human-animal bond through research, education, rescue and rehabilitation.

​

Final Frontier Rescue Project

Working with non-profit rescue organizations, our group of volunteers and advocates have been saving rehabilitatable and manageable dogs in danger of being killed at Austin Animal Center in Austin, TX since September 2016. By saving the "Final Frontier Dogs"--the 1-2% still being killed--we can develop best practices for the nation's shelters. As of August 31, 2018, we are a 501(c)3 nonprofit, the Indie Institute, with a mission to improve animal sheltering and welfare and promote the human-animal bond through research, education, rescue and rehabilitation. Final Frontier Rescue Project is the rescue and rehabilitation project of the larger nonprofit organization.

How You Can Help

You don't have to live in Texas or be able to physically spend time with the dogs to do this. If you love one or more of these dogs, you are welcome! "Job" description: With your team members, create a goal: foster, adopter, or fundraising for care/training.
1. Plan and implement a marketing strategy using photos/video and information provided here (or get more images and info yourself by visiting with the dog). Your strategy might include adoption events, flyers, Craigslist, Nextdoor, Facebook, Instagram, word-of-mouth, Dodo, Huffington Post, local media, etc.
2. Set a requested adoption fee.
3. Use or adapt this foster/adoption application: https://goo.gl/forms/nG0VivcBDxIl8je02
4. Do initial screening of potential fosters/adopters.
5. Let us know of good prospects.
6. Help with home visit and actual adoption if you choose.
7. Celebrate success!
8. As a group, reflect on and learn from the experience so we can get better at finding good homes quickly.
